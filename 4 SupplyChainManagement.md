### Module 1: Procurement Management
Manages the purchasing of goods and services to ensure cost-effectiveness and timely delivery.
1.  **Purchase Requisition:** Internal requests for goods, approval workflows, and budget checking.
2.  **Request for Quotation (RFQ):** Creation and distribution of RFQs to multiple vendors, and comparison of vendor quotes.
3.  **Purchase Order (PO) Management:** Generation, approval, amendment, and cancellation of purchase orders.
4.  **Vendor Portal:** A self-service portal for suppliers to view POs, acknowledge orders, and update shipment status.
5.  **Invoice Reconciliation:** Three-way matching of PO, Goods Receipt Note (GRN), and Vendor Invoice.

### Module 2: Supplier Relationship Management (SRM)
Focuses on managing interactions with current and potential suppliers.
1.  **Supplier Onboarding:** Registration forms, qualification questionnaires, and due diligence checks.
2.  **Supplier Scorecard:** Performance tracking based on delivery time, quality, price, and responsiveness.
3.  **Contract Management:** Storage of supplier contracts, renewal alerts, and terms & conditions tracking.
4.  **Supplier Catalog Management:** Management of standard item catalogs provided by specific suppliers.
5.  **Risk Management:** Assessment of supplier risk factors (financial, geo-political, compliance).

### Module 3: Inventory Management
Tracks and manages stock levels to prevent stockouts and overstock situations.
1.  **Stock Control:** Real-time tracking of stock quantities, batch numbers, and serial numbers.
2.  **Warehouse Transfer:** Management of stock movement between different warehouse locations.
3.  **Stock Adjustment:** Handling of inventory write-offs, damages, and cycle count corrections.
4.  **Reorder Point Automation:** Automated alerts and PO generation when stock falls below defined safety levels.
5.  **Inventory Valuation:** Calculation of stock value using methods like FIFO, LIFO, and Weighted Average.

### Module 4: Warehouse Management System (WMS)
Controls the physical operations within the warehouse.
1.  **Inbound Operations:** Dock scheduling, receiving, and put-away strategies.
2.  **Outbound Operations:** Picking strategies (wave, batch, zone), packing, and shipping label generation.
3.  **Bin/Location Management:** Mapping of warehouse layout and optimization of storage space.
4.  **Cycle Counting:** Scheduled counting of specific inventory sections without halting operations.
5.  **Yard Management:** Tracking of trucks and trailers within the warehouse yard.

### Module 5: Order Management System (OMS)
Centralizes the processing of orders from various sales channels.
1.  **Order Capture:** Manual entry or automated import of orders from e-commerce/ERP systems.
2.  **Order Validation:** Checking credit limits, inventory availability, and fraud detection.
3.  **Order Allocation:** Logic-based assignment of orders to specific fulfillment centers.
4.  **Backorder Management:** Handling orders for items currently out of stock.
5.  **Customer Notifications:** Automated emails/SMS for order confirmation, shipping, and delivery.

### Module 6: Transportation Management System (TMS)
Plans, executes, and optimizes the physical movement of goods.
1.  **Route Planning:** Optimization of delivery routes to reduce fuel consumption and time.
2.  **Freight Audit & Payment:** Verification of freight bills against contracts and processing payments.
3.  **Carrier Management:** Management of 3rd party logistics (3PL) partners and rate cards.
4.  **Shipment Tracking:** Real-time GPS tracking and status updates for in-transit shipments.
5.  **Load Optimization:** Planning how to best load cargo into containers or trucks (cube utilization).

### Module 7: Demand Planning & Forecasting
Uses data to predict future demand and align supply accordingly.
1.  **Sales Forecasting:** Statistical forecasting based on historical sales data and trends.
2.  **Seasonality Analysis:** Adjustment of plans based on seasonal peaks and promotional events.
3.  **Demand Sensing:** Short-term forecasting using real-time market signals.
4.  **Collaborative Planning:** Interface for sales, marketing, and finance teams to input consensus data.
5.  **Safety Stock Calculation:** Dynamic calculation of buffer stock based on demand variability.

### Module 8: Manufacturing / Production
Manages the transformation of raw materials into finished goods (for manufacturing tenants).
1.  **Bill of Materials (BOM):** Definition of raw materials, sub-assemblies, and quantities required.
2.  **Production Scheduling:** Planning of production runs based on capacity and material availability.
3.  **Work Order Management:** Issuance and tracking of work orders on the shop floor.
4.  **Material Resource Planning (MRP):** Calculation of material requirements based on production plans.
5.  **Shop Floor Control:** Tracking of machine time, labor time, and production progress.

### Module 9: Quality Management System (QMS)
Ensures products meet quality standards and regulatory requirements.
1.  **Quality Inspection:** Definition of inspection criteria for incoming and outgoing goods.
2.  **Non-Conformance Reports (NCR):** Documentation of products failing quality checks.
3.  **Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA):** Management of workflows to address root causes of defects.
4.  **Audit Management:** Scheduling and execution of internal and external audits.
5.  **Certificate of Analysis (CoA):** Generation of compliance certificates for shipped batches.

### Module 10: Returns Management (Reverse Logistics)
Handles the return of products from customers back to the warehouse.
1.  **Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA):** Approval workflows for customer return requests.
2.  **Refund Processing:** Integration with finance to trigger refunds or store credits.
3.  **Disposition Management:** Decision logic for repair, refurbish, scrap, or restock returned items.
4.  **Return Portal:** Customer-facing interface to request returns and print labels.
5.  **Warranty Claims:** Management of warranty claims against suppliers or manufacturers.

### Module 11: Supply Chain Analytics
Provides insights into performance and efficiency.
1.  **Inventory Dashboards:** Visual reporting on turnover rates, dead stock, and aging inventory.
2.  **Procurement Analytics:** Spend analysis, supplier performance trends, and cost-saving opportunities.
3.  **Logistics KPIs:** On-time delivery rates, freight cost per unit, and vehicle utilization reports.
4.  **Financial Reporting:** Gross margin analysis and supply chain cost breakdowns.
5.  **Predictive Analytics:** AI-driven predictions for potential disruptions or demand spikes.

### Module 12: Contract & Compliance Management
Manages legal agreements and regulatory adherence across the chain.
1.  **Contract Repository:** Centralized storage for logistics contracts, supplier agreements, and NDAs.
2.  **Compliance Tracking:** Monitoring adherence to regulations (e.g., FDA, HazMat, GDPR).
3.  **Trade Documentation:** Generation and management of import/export documents (Bill of Lading, CI).
4.  **License Management:** Tracking of import/export licenses and expiration dates.
5.  **Sustainability Tracking:** Carbon footprint reporting and ethical sourcing compliance.

### Module 13: Asset Management
Manages the physical assets used to run the supply chain (trucks, forklifts, machinery).
1.  **Asset Registry:** Database of all physical assets with specifications and location.
2.  **Preventive Maintenance:** Scheduling of regular maintenance tasks to prevent breakdowns.
3.  **Breakdown Maintenance:** Logging of unplanned repairs and downtime tracking.
4.  **Spare Parts Inventory:** Management of inventory required for machine maintenance.
5.  **Asset Depreciation:** Financial tracking of asset value over time.

### Module 14: Labor Management
Optimizes the workforce within warehouses and logistics.
1.  **Labor Planning:** Forecasting labor requirements based on inbound/outbound volume.
2.  **Time & Attendance:** Clock-in/out functionality and attendance tracking.
3.  **Task Assignment:** Assigning specific tasks (picking, packing) to individual workers.
4.  **Performance Tracking:** Measuring worker productivity (units per hour) and accuracy.
5.  **Payroll Integration:** Exporting labor data for payroll processing.

### Module 15: Cold Chain Management
Specialized module for temperature-sensitive logistics (Pharma/Food).
1.  **Temperature Monitoring:** Integration with IoT sensors to track temperature in real-time.
2.  **Excursion Management:** Alerts and workflows when temperature deviates from safe ranges.
3.  **Cold Storage Inventory:** Specific tracking for items requiring refrigeration or freezing.
4.  **Compliance Reporting:** Automated generation of reports for health and safety audits.
5.  **Maintenance of Reefers:** Specific maintenance schedules for refrigerated containers/units.

### Module 16: Customer Portal
A dedicated interface for clients (B2B or B2C) to interact with the supply chain.
1.  **Order Tracking:** Real-time visibility of order status and location.
2.  **Account Management:** Management of user profiles, addresses, and payment methods.
3.  **Document Retrieval:** Access to invoices, POD (Proof of Delivery), and contracts.
4.  **Support Ticketing:** System for raising complaints or queries regarding orders.
5.  **Catalog Browsing:** Viewing available products and current stock levels.

### Module 17: Third-Party Logistics (3PL) Management
Specifically for tenants acting as logistics providers for other companies.
1.  **Client Billing:** Automated billing based on storage volume, transactions, or weight handled.
2.  **Client Inventory Segregation:** Strict separation of inventory belonging to different clients.
3.  **SLA Management:** Monitoring Service Level Agreements (SLA) for each client.
4.  **Client Integration:** APIs to sync data with the client's own ERP systems.
5.  **Warehouse Rental Management:** Billing logic for dedicated vs. shared warehouse space.

### Module 18: Finance & Accounting Integration
Connects supply chain operations with financial records.
1.  **Accounts Payable:** Management of money owed to vendors and carriers.
2.  **Accounts Receivable:** Management of payments due from customers.
3.  **Landed Cost Calculation:** Calculation of the total cost of a product including shipping, customs, and insurance.
4.  **Budgeting:** Setting and tracking budgets for different supply chain departments.
5.  **Tax Management:** Calculation and handling of sales tax, VAT, and customs duties.

### Module 19: Integration & API Gateway
Manages connectivity with external systems.
1.  **ERP Integration:** Connectors for SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, or Microsoft Dynamics.
2.  **E-commerce Integration:** Connectors for Shopify, Magento, WooCommerce, or Amazon.
3.  **IoT Gateway:** Ingestion of data from RFID tags, barcode scanners, and sensors.
4.  **EDI Management:** Electronic Data Interchange for standardized B2B communication.
5.  **Webhooks:** Configurable triggers to send data to external applications upon specific events.

### Module 20: Multi-Tenant Administration (Super Admin)
The technical backbone enabling the SaaS/Multi-tenant nature of the application.
1.  **Tenant Onboarding:** Automated provisioning of new tenant environments and databases.
2.  **Subscription Management:** Management of pricing plans, billing cycles, and feature access per tenant.
3.  **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Granular permission settings for users within each tenant.
4.  **Theme & Customization:** White-labeling options allowing tenants to apply their branding/logo.
5.  **Audit Logs & Security:** System-wide logs for data access, changes, and security incident monitoring.
