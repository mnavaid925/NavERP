# Research — Module 3: HRM, Sub-module 3.20 Continuous Feedback (hrm-continuous-feedback)

Third Performance-Management sub-module (3.18 Goal Setting → 3.19 Performance Review → **3.20 Continuous
Feedback** → 3.21 Performance Improvement). Scope is the *ongoing/informal* layer: real-time kudos/praise/
constructive feedback, 1:1 meetings (agenda/notes/action items), a given-vs-received feedback dashboard, and
anonymous/safe feedback channels. PIP/warning-letters/coaching notes are explicitly OUT of scope — deferred to
3.21.

## Feature catalog by sub-module

All 4 bullets below sit under **NavERP.md §3.20 Continuous Feedback**. Since 3.20 is a single flat sub-module
(no 3.20.1/3.20.2 numbering in NavERP.md), features are grouped by its 4 named bullets instead.

### 3.20 · Real-time Feedback — Kudos, appreciation, constructive feedback
- **Give feedback (any employee → any employee, any time)** — the core write path every product has: a short
  message from a giver to a receiver, not gated by a review cycle. Seen in: Lattice, Leapsome, BetterWorks,
  Reflektive, Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, greytHR, Keka. Priority: **must-have**. Spine: new 3.20-owned
  `Feedback` row; `giver`/`receiver` both FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile` (never a raw Party/User — mirrors
  `PerformanceReview.subject/reviewer`). Buildable now.
- **Feedback type / tone (praise vs. constructive vs. request)** — NavERP.md's bullet names three tones
  explicitly ("Kudos, appreciation, constructive feedback"). Seen in: Keka ("public recognition... private
  feedback for growth"), Lattice, 15Five (High Fives are recognition-only; separate "Check-in" feedback is
  developmental). Priority: **must-have**. Spine: `feedback_type` choice field on the new `Feedback` model
  (e.g. `kudos` / `appreciation` / `constructive` / `question`). Buildable now.
- **Public vs. private visibility per feedback item** — Keka and Matter both let the giver choose
  public-feed vs. private-to-receiver at send time (distinct from anonymity — the giver's identity is always
  known here, only the *audience* is scoped). Seen in: Keka, Matter, Small Improvements. Priority: **must-have**.
  Spine: `visibility` choice (`private` / `team` / `public`) on `Feedback`. Buildable now.
- **Value/company-values tagging** — Bonusly and Kudos both structurally tie every recognition to a company
  value; Small Improvements attaches a "badge" instead. Seen in: Bonusly, Kudos, Small Improvements, Matter.
  Priority: **common** (near-universal in the recognition-specialist tier, less so in generic
  HRIS-embedded feedback like Workday/SAP). Spine: needs a small tenant catalog — the optional
  `KudosBadge`/value-tag model recommended below; FK from `Feedback`.
- **Social recognition feed (public wall, likes/comments)** — Bonusly's "social recognition feed", Kudos's
  "social feed... like a social media timeline" with likes/comments, Small Improvements' "Praise Wall". Seen
  in: Bonusly, Kudos, Small Improvements, 15Five (High Fives Feed), Officevibe, Engagedly. Priority:
  **common**. Spine: reuses `Feedback` rows filtered `visibility != private` for a list view; likes/comments as
  a first-class model is a **defer** (out of one sub-module pass — see Deferred).
  Buildable-now core (the feed query); comments/reactions integration/later.
- **Gamification — points, leaderboards, redeemable rewards catalog** — Bonusly's points→gift-card/charity
  catalog, Kudos's leaderboards, Matter's "coins." Seen in: Bonusly, Kudos, Matter. Priority: **nice-to-have**
  (a differentiator tier feature, not table-stakes across the surveyed set — Lattice/Leapsome/Keka/SAP/Workday
  have none of it). Spine: would need a new `RewardCatalog`/`PointLedger` pair — **defer**, integration/later
  (redemption typically needs a payment/gift-card vendor).
- **Milestone auto-celebrations (birthdays, work anniversaries)** — Kudos's automated milestone cards. Seen
  in: Kudos, Bonusly. Priority: **nice-to-have**. Spine: derivable from `EmployeeProfile.date_of_birth` /
  `core.Employment` hire date — **defer** (a scheduled-job/notification feature, not a new table; low priority
  for this pass).
- **Request feedback (pull, not just push)** — Workday's "manager requests feedback on any team member" Inbox
  task; SAP SuccessFactors' "Requests Sent" tab; greytHR's explicit "request feedback from other employees"
  action; Reflektive's request workflow. Seen in: Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, greytHR, Reflektive, Culture
  Amp ("employees can proactively seek specific input"). Priority: **must-have** — every enterprise/HRIS-native
  implementation has it, and it's the mechanism that makes "given vs. received" symmetric with "requested."
  Spine: a `status` field on `Feedback` (`requested` / `given`) plus a self-referential requester link, OR a
  thin `requested_by`/`is_response_to` FK on the same model — see build-scope recommendation below (fold into
  `Feedback`, don't spin a separate model). Buildable now.
- **Feedback tied to work — project/goal/review context** — Lattice ties feedback "to specific work, projects,
  or behaviors"; Leapsome/BetterWorks pull recent feedback into the review and 1:1 agenda. NavERP already has
  the two natural anchors: 3.18 `Objective` and 3.19 `PerformanceReview`. Seen in: Lattice, Leapsome,
  BetterWorks, 15Five (High Fives surfaced inside review-writing). Priority: **common**. Spine: **reuse** —
  two nullable FKs on `Feedback` (`related_objective` → `hrm.Objective`, `related_review` →
  `hrm.PerformanceReview`), both optional so plain kudos need neither. Buildable now.

### 3.20 · 1:1 Meetings — Meeting scheduling, notes, action items
- **Recurring 1:1 schedule between a manager and a direct report** — the universal spine of every product
  surveyed. Seen in: all 14 (Lattice, Culture Amp, 15Five, Leapsome, Officevibe, Small Improvements,
  BetterWorks, Trakstar, Reflektive, SAP SuccessFactors, Workday-integrated tools, Darwinbox, Keka, greytHR).
  Priority: **must-have**. Spine: new `OneOnOneMeeting` row; `manager`/`employee` both FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`
  (manager resolved from the existing DERIVED `EmployeeProfile.manager` reporting line established in 3.19 —
  no new manager-lookup table). Buildable now.
- **Shared/collaborative agenda — talking points contributed by both sides** — Culture Amp, Small Improvements
  ("Talking Points... both participants can contribute"), Keka ("shared talking points"), SAP SuccessFactors
  ("topics for discussion"). Priority: **must-have**. Spine: a text field on `OneOnOneMeeting`
  (`agenda`/`talking_points`) editable by either party pre-meeting — simplest one-pass shape; a fully
  real-time collaborative multi-line agenda editor is **defer** (integration/later, needs live-collab infra).
- **Meeting notes — shared vs. private** — Small Improvements ("both parties can add their thoughts
  afterward... all notes in one place"); Culture Amp's newer "private bookmarks... remaining private for
  personal reference"; SAP SuccessFactors "Save and Finish... captured in the meeting history." Priority:
  **must-have** for shared notes, **common** for a private-notes split. Spine: `shared_notes` (visible to both)
  plus a `manager_private_notes` field on `OneOnOneMeeting`, mirroring the 3.19 `PerformanceReview.private_notes`
  confidentiality precedent (manager-only, never rendered on the employee-facing view). Buildable now.
- **Action items with owner + due date** — explicitly named in NavERP.md's bullet; universal across products
  (Small Improvements "Action items to record and track... to-dos", Culture Amp, Lattice, BetterWorks,
  greytHR, Keka). Priority: **must-have**. Spine: new **child** table `MeetingActionItem` FK'd to
  `OneOnOneMeeting`, `owner` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`, `due_date`, `status` (open/done). Buildable now.
- **Meeting history / past-1:1 record kept on the employee's timeline** — Keka ("track past discussions"),
  SAP SuccessFactors ("meeting history for future reference"), Small Improvements. Priority: **must-have**
  (this is just "don't delete past 1:1 rows" — an ordering/list-view concern, not a new model). Spine:
  **reuse** — `OneOnOneMeeting.objects.filter(employee=...).order_by('-scheduled_at')` is the history; no
  separate history table needed (mirrors how `GoalCheckIn` is itself the append-only history for KeyResults).
  Buildable now.
- **Calendar sync (Google/Outlook) + automated reminders** — Keka explicitly ("sync with Google or Outlook,
  automate reminders"), Officevibe/Reflektive (Slack/Teams nudges). Priority: **nice-to-have**. Spine: none
  needed for the data model (a `scheduled_at` datetime field is enough to build on); the actual calendar
  push/notification is **integration/later**.
- **Pre-meeting pulse / engagement check-in** — Trakstar's "Engagement Check-In... short questionnaire before
  their 1-on-1"; Darwinbox's post-meeting "rate this conversation." Priority: **nice-to-have** /
  **differentiator** (only 2 of 14 products do this). Spine: would need a small rating field or a link to a
  pulse-survey mechanism — **defer**, folds better into a future engagement-survey sub-module than into 3.20's
  core 1:1 model.
- **Bulk-invite / meeting-channel setup at scale** — SAP SuccessFactors' "bulk invitations for 1:1 meeting
  channels." Priority: **nice-to-have** (an admin-efficiency feature, not a data-model concern). Buildable now
  as a view-level convenience later; not a modeling requirement for this pass.
- **1:1 wired to goals/OKRs** — BetterWorks "1:1 templates connected to OKRs," Lattice ("talking points pulled
  from current goals"), 15Five ("OKR progress" in agenda items). Priority: **common**. Spine: **reuse** — an
  optional `related_objective` FK on `OneOnOneMeeting` → `hrm.Objective`, same pattern as the Feedback model's
  optional goal link above. Buildable now.

### 3.20 · Feedback Dashboard — Given/received feedback summary
- **Given vs. Received split view** — SAP SuccessFactors' exact three-tab model (Received / Given / Requests
  Sent) is the clearest documented precedent for NavERP's bullet wording. Seen in: SAP SuccessFactors, 15Five
  (High Fives given/received context inside reviews), Officevibe (Good Vibes given/received trend), Bonusly.
  Priority: **must-have**. Spine: **computed view, not a new table** — two annotated querysets over the same
  `Feedback` model (`Feedback.objects.filter(receiver=emp)` vs. `filter(giver=emp)`), exactly how 3.18's
  `Objective.progress_pct`/3.19's `PerformanceReview.overall_rating` are Python-derived properties rather than
  stored columns. Buildable now — no schema needed beyond the `Feedback` model itself.
- **Feedback velocity / frequency trend** — Officevibe's "Good Vibes report... track recognition trends by
  team or time period"; Leapsome's admin reminders "when feedback or praise hasn't been given" (implies a
  tracked cadence). Priority: **common**. Spine: reuse — a `created_at`-bucketed count over `Feedback`
  (dashboard aggregation, no new table). Buildable now.
- **Sentiment / type breakdown (kudos vs. constructive vs. requests, by team/department)** — Officevibe/Peakon's
  team-level segmentation; SuccessFactors' Requests-Sent tracking. Priority: **common**. Spine: reuse —
  `Feedback.objects.values('feedback_type').annotate(Count(...))`, optionally joined through
  `EmployeeProfile.employment.org_unit` for a department cut. Buildable now.
- **Reminders when feedback/praise cadence lapses** — Leapsome's explicit admin-configurable nudge workflow.
  Priority: **nice-to-have**. Spine: none (a scheduled-notification feature) — **defer**, integration/later
  (ties to the core `Notification` model from Module 0, not a 3.20-owned table).
- **AI-synthesized sentiment insights on feedback text** — Peakon's "GenAI-synthesized sentiment insights."
  Priority: **differentiator** (only the enterprise-listening tier does this). **Defer** — integration/later
  (needs an LLM call, out of a single Django modeling pass).

### 3.20 · Anonymous Feedback — Safe feedback channels
- **Anonymous submission (giver identity withheld from receiver)** — the defining Peakon/Officevibe
  capability, and directly named in NavERP.md's bullet. Seen in: Peakon (pulse surveys + "anonymous
  commenting"), Officevibe ("anonymous feedback... peer-to-peer recognition"), Darwinbox (pulse surveys).
  Priority: **must-have**. Spine: **reuse the exact pattern 3.19 already established** —
  `PerformanceReview.is_anonymous` masks the reviewer on the subject-facing view while the FK still exists in
  the DB (auditable, never truly untraceable — admin/HR can still see it). Add the identical `is_anonymous`
  boolean to the new `Feedback` model rather than inventing a separate anonymous-only table. Buildable now.
- **Two-way anonymous reply channel** (receiver can respond without the system revealing the original
  giver's identity to them) — noted as a best-practice pattern in anonymous-feedback-tool comparisons
  ("manager can respond to anonymous feedback... without revealing the sender's identity"). Priority:
  **nice-to-have** / **differentiator**. Spine: would need a reply thread that preserves the masking
  asymmetrically — **defer** (adds real complexity — a self-referencing `Feedback.in_reply_to` with
  per-viewer masking logic is a good candidate for a *later* enhancement pass, not this one).
- **Safe-channel access control (who can see an anonymous item)** — mirrors 3.19's confidentiality design point
  exactly: subject/HR-admin-only visibility, giver identity redacted everywhere except an audit trail. Seen
  in: Peakon, Officevibe, and implicit in every anonymous-review product surveyed. Priority: **must-have**.
  Spine: **reuse** the 3.19 view-authorization pattern (`_can_view_review`-style helper) — a
  `_can_view_feedback(user, feedback)` helper gating detail/list access, with the `giver` FK never rendered
  when `is_anonymous=True` except to the receiver's manager/tenant-admin. Buildable now (view-layer logic, no
  new table).
- **Anonymous pulse surveys (org-wide sentiment, not 1:1 feedback)** — Peakon's core product *is* this; it's a
  materially different feature (aggregate survey instrument vs. one-to-one feedback message). Priority:
  **differentiator**, and **out of scope for 3.20** as modeled — NavERP.md's 3.20 bullet says "Anonymous
  Feedback — Safe feedback channels" (singular feedback items), not a survey engine. **Defer** to a future
  engagement-survey sub-module/module if NavERP ever adds one; do not conflate with `Feedback.is_anonymous`.

## Recommended build scope (this pass — 4 models)

- **`Feedback`** [FBK-] — `TenantNumbered`. Fields: `giver` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile` (nullable/blank when
  `is_anonymous=True` masks it on render — mirrors `PerformanceReview.reviewer` + `is_anonymous`),
  `receiver` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`, `feedback_type` choice (`kudos`/`appreciation`/`constructive`/`request`),
  `visibility` choice (`private`/`team`/`public`), `message` TextField, `is_anonymous` BooleanField (default
  False — direct reuse of the 3.19 masking pattern), `badge` FK to the optional `KudosBadge` catalog (nullable),
  `related_objective` FK `hrm.Objective` (nullable — 3.18 link), `related_review` FK `hrm.PerformanceReview`
  (nullable — 3.19 link), `requested_from`/`is_response_to` self-FK (nullable — folds the "request feedback"
  pull workflow into the same model instead of a second table, so `status` can simply be `requested` vs. the
  default given state). Justified by: Real-time Feedback (all bullets above) + both dashboard bullets (given/
  received/requested is just three queryset filters over this one table) + anonymous-channel bullets. Reuses
  `hrm.EmployeeProfile`, optionally `hrm.Objective`, `hrm.PerformanceReview`.
- **`OneOnOneMeeting`** [1O1- or OOM-] — `TenantNumbered`. Fields: `manager` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`,
  `employee` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`, `scheduled_at` DateTimeField, `status` choice
  (`scheduled`/`completed`/`cancelled`), `agenda`/`talking_points` TextField (shared, editable pre-meeting by
  either party), `shared_notes` TextField (both can see post-meeting), `manager_private_notes` TextField
  (manager-only, never rendered on the employee-facing detail — direct reuse of the
  `PerformanceReview.private_notes` confidentiality precedent), `related_objective` FK `hrm.Objective`
  (nullable). Justified by: 1:1 Meetings bullet (scheduling/agenda/notes) + the private-vs-shared-notes
  confidentiality research finding (Culture Amp bookmarks, Small Improvements). Reuses `hrm.EmployeeProfile`,
  optionally `hrm.Objective`. Its own querysets (`filter(employee=X).order_by('-scheduled_at')`) serve the
  "meeting history" feature with no extra table.
- **`MeetingActionItem`** [MAI-] — `TenantNumbered`, child of `OneOnOneMeeting`. Fields: `meeting` FK
  `hrm.OneOnOneMeeting`, `description` TextField, `owner` FK `hrm.EmployeeProfile`, `due_date` DateField
  (nullable), `status` choice (`open`/`done`), `completed_at` DateTimeField (nullable, set on status change —
  mirrors how other HRM child rows timestamp their terminal state). Justified by: the "action items" clause
  explicitly named in NavERP.md's 1:1 Meetings bullet, and every surveyed product's action-item tracking
  (Small Improvements, Culture Amp, Lattice, greytHR, Keka). Child pattern mirrors `KeyResult`→`Objective` and
  `ReviewRating`→`PerformanceReview`.
- **`KudosBadge`** [optional, small catalog — not auto-numbered] — `TenantOwned` only (no `NUMBER_PREFIX`, same
  pattern as `JobGrade`/`GoalPeriod`/`ReviewCycle`: identified by `name`, `unique_together=(tenant, name)`).
  Fields: `name`, `description`, `icon`/`color` (free-text for a UI chip), `linked_value` CharField (optional
  free-text tag to a company value, per Bonusly/Kudos/Small Improvements), `is_active` BooleanField. Justified
  by: the values/badge-tagging feature seen in 4 of 14 products (Bonusly, Kudos, Small Improvements, Matter).
  Marked **optional** — if the pass needs to trim to 3 models, drop this and fold a single free-text
  `badge_label` CharField directly onto `Feedback` instead (loses the reusable catalog/consistency benefit but
  keeps the feature).

**Feedback Dashboard is explicitly NOT a 5th model** — per the research, every leading product's dashboard
(SAP SuccessFactors' Given/Received/Requested tabs, Officevibe's Good Vibes report, 15Five's given/received
context) is a **computed view** over the `Feedback` table, exactly mirroring how 3.18's `progress_pct` and
3.19's `overall_rating` are Python `@property`/queryset aggregations rather than stored columns. Build it as a
dashboard view/template reading annotated querysets over `Feedback`, not a new table.

## Deferred (later passes / integrations)

- **Points/leaderboards/redeemable-rewards catalog** (Bonusly, Kudos, Matter coins) — needs a `PointLedger` +
  `RewardCatalog` pair and typically a payment/gift-card vendor integration; a differentiator, not table-stakes.
- **Public social feed with likes/comments on feedback items** — the *feed query* (public `Feedback` rows) is
  buildable now; a first-class `FeedbackReaction`/`FeedbackComment` model is deferred to keep this pass to 4
  models.
- **Milestone auto-celebrations** (birthdays, work anniversaries) — derivable from existing
  `EmployeeProfile`/`core.Employment` dates via a scheduled job; no new table, just not in this pass's view
  layer yet.
- **Reminders when feedback/1:1 cadence lapses** (Leapsome) — belongs on the core `Notification` model
  (Module 0), triggered by a scheduled task reading `Feedback`/`OneOnOneMeeting` recency; not a 3.20-owned
  table.
- **AI-synthesized sentiment insights** (Peakon) — requires an LLM integration; out of a single Django
  modeling pass.
- **Calendar sync (Google/Outlook) for 1:1 scheduling** (Keka) — `OneOnOneMeeting.scheduled_at` is enough of a
  data model to build the integration against later; the sync itself is external.
- **Pre-meeting engagement pulse / post-meeting "rate this conversation"** (Trakstar, Darwinbox) — a
  differentiator seen in only 2 of 14 products; better suited to a future engagement-survey capability than
  bolted onto `OneOnOneMeeting` in this pass.
- **Two-way anonymous reply threads** (preserving asymmetric masking) — real complexity beyond a simple
  `is_anonymous` boolean; a candidate enhancement to `Feedback` in a later pass, not this one.
- **Org-wide anonymous pulse-survey engine** (Peakon's core product) — a materially different instrument
  (aggregate survey vs. one-to-one feedback message); out of scope for NavERP.md's 3.20 bullet wording and not
  proposed as a model here.
- **Bulk-invite to recurring 1:1 channels at scale** (SAP SuccessFactors) — an admin UI/UX convenience over the
  existing `OneOnOneMeeting` model, not a modeling requirement.
